**Quality criteria** (Premium documentary thumbnail aesthetic):

**1. CINEMATIC QUALITY (0-3 points)** 🎯 MOST IMPORTANT
- Does it look like a frame from a prestige documentary or film?
- Intentional lighting with clear direction (not flat, not generic "dramatic")
- Film-like color grading — muted, graded, not oversaturated
- Organic texture (film grain, atmosphere) vs. sterile digital smoothness
Score: 3=could be a movie poster, 2=feels professional, 1=looks AI-generated, 0=obviously AI

**2. VISUAL INTRIGUE (0-2 points)** - What makes someone stop scrolling
- Creates genuine curiosity through composition, subject, or mood
- NOT through shock, exaggeration, or clickbait tactics
- Veritasium framework: High info gap + low misleading = ideal
- The image raises a question the viewer wants answered
Score: 2=genuinely compelling, 1=mildly interesting, 0=generic or try-hard

**3. COMPOSITION & RESTRAINT (0-2 points)**
- Clean, intentional composition with breathing room (negative space)
- ONE clear focal point — not cluttered with competing elements
- Sophisticated use of depth (foreground/background separation, bokeh)
- Feels composed by a cinematographer, not assembled by AI
Score: 2=masterful restraint, 1=acceptable, 0=cluttered or generic

**4. PREMIUM FEEL (0-2 points)**
- Would this fit on BBC Earth, Wired, National Geographic, or Apple's website?
- Avoids childish, cartoonish, or over-the-top imagery
- Color palette is cohesive and intentional
- Specific, not generic — has a distinct mood and identity
Score: 2=premium/sophisticated, 1=decent, 0=cheap or cringe

**5. THUMBNAIL-SPECIFIC (0-1 point)**
- Readable at YouTube thumbnail size (320x180px)
- Distinct from similar videos (not generic stock)
- Clear visual story without needing the title
Score: 1=works perfectly at small size, 0=loses impact when small

**SCORE 0-10**.
- 3-5: Typical AI output (oversaturated, busy, generic)
- 6-7: Decent but still feels "generated"
- 8-9: Premium — could pass for real photography/cinematography
- 10: Exceptional — genuinely compelling, zero AI tells

**RED FLAGS (auto-deduct 2 points each):**
- Oversaturated neon colors
- Multiple competing focal points
- Clickbait-style shock faces or exaggerated expressions
- Perfect digital symmetry (AI tell)
- Cliché imagery (glowing brains, matrix rain, floating holograms)
