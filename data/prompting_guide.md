# YouTube Thumbnail Prompting Guide — Premium Documentary Aesthetic

This guide produces thumbnails that feel like frames from prestige documentaries and cinema, not typical AI-generated YouTube content.

## ⛔ CRITICAL: NO TEXT IN IMAGES

**NEVER include text, words, letters, numbers, logos, watermarks, titles, labels, captions, or any written content in image prompts.** Text will be added separately. Every prompt MUST end with "no text, no words, no letters, no logos" to reinforce this.

## The Aesthetic North Star

**Think**: BBC Earth × Wired × Apple. Premium, cinematic, intentional. Every frame should feel like it was composed by a cinematographer, not generated by AI.

## Prompt Structure

```
[CINEMATIC STYLE/CAMERA] [SUBJECT] [LIGHTING DIRECTION] [ATMOSPHERE/MOOD] [COLOR GRADE] [COMPOSITION], 16:9, cinematic color grading, film grain, no text, no words, no letters, no logos
```

## 1. Cinematic Language (USE THESE)

### Camera & Lens
- "Shot on ARRI Alexa" / "Shot on RED Komodo" (cinematic digital)
- "Kodak Portra 400" / "Kodak Vision3 500T" (film stocks for organic feel)
- "85mm f/1.4 shallow depth of field" (intimate portraits)
- "24mm wide angle" (environmental/landscape)
- "Macro lens, extreme close-up" (texture and detail)
- "Anamorphic lens flare" (cinematic widescreen feel)

### Lighting
- "Golden hour sidelight" — warm, directional, long shadows
- "Overcast diffused light" — soft, even, moody
- "Single practical light source" — lamp, screen glow, candle
- "Chiaroscuro" — dramatic light/shadow contrast (Rembrandt, Caravaggio)
- "Backlit silhouette" — subject outlined against bright background
- "Blue hour twilight" — cool, contemplative atmosphere
- "Volumetric light through fog/dust" — atmosphere and depth

### Color Grading
- "Teal and orange cinema grade" — classic blockbuster look
- "Desaturated earth tones" — muted, natural, grounded
- "Monochromatic with one accent color" — sophisticated restraint
- "Cool blue shadows, warm highlights" — split toning
- "Faded film look, lifted blacks" — vintage cinema
- "High contrast black and white" — dramatic, timeless

### Atmosphere
- "Atmospheric haze" / "Morning fog" / "Dust particles in light"
- "Rain-slicked surfaces reflecting light"
- "Shallow depth of field, bokeh background"
- "Film grain texture" — adds organic, non-digital feel
- "Negative space" — breathing room, not cluttered

## 2. Composition Principles

- **ONE focal point** — the eye should know exactly where to go
- **Breathing room** — leave 30-40% negative space, don't fill every pixel
- **Rule of thirds** — subject off-center for dynamic composition
- **Scale contrast** — tiny human in vast landscape, or extreme close-up detail
- **Leading lines** — architecture, roads, light beams guiding the eye
- **Depth layers** — foreground blur, sharp subject, atmospheric background

## 3. What Makes It Premium (Not AI-Looking)

### DO
- Specific camera/lens references (grounds the look in real photography)
- Natural, motivated lighting (light that has a logical source)
- Muted, graded colors (not straight-out-of-camera saturation)
- Film grain or subtle texture (breaks up digital smoothness)
- Environmental context (subject IN a real-feeling space)
- Asymmetric composition (perfectly centered = stock photo)
- Imperfection — slight motion blur, dust, lens imperfections

### DON'T
- Perfect symmetry (looks AI-generated)
- Oversaturated colors (screams "I used the vibrance slider")
- Multiple competing focal points (cluttered = amateur)
- Generic "dramatic lighting" without specifying direction
- Floating objects with no context or ground plane
- Perfectly clean/sterile environments (too digital)
- Cliché poses (arms crossed, pointing, hands on hips)

## 4. Subject-Specific Guidance

### People
- Candid over posed — caught in a moment, not performing
- Environmental portraits — person IN their world, not isolated
- Partial faces, profiles, silhouettes — more cinematic than head-on
- Real emotion over performed emotion — contemplation over shock

### Technology/AI Topics
- Physical metaphors over digital ones — a single chess piece, an hourglass, a mirror
- Human scale — technology's impact shown through people and spaces
- Intimate angles on hardware — macro shots of circuits as abstract landscapes
- Empty spaces where humans should be — chairs, rooms, cities

### Nature/Science
- Vast scale with human reference point
- Extreme macro revealing hidden worlds
- Time-of-day specificity — not "dramatic sky" but "5am pre-dawn blue"
- Weather and atmosphere as mood setters

## 5. Example Prompt Patterns

**Intimate Portrait:**
"Close-up portrait, 85mm f/1.4, shallow depth of field, subject looking slightly off-camera with contemplative expression, warm golden hour sidelight from left, dark moody background, teal and orange color grade, film grain, 16:9, no text, no words, no letters, no logos"

**Epic Scale:**
"Vast aerial shot, lone figure standing at the edge of an enormous canyon, blue hour twilight, atmospheric haze in the valley below, desaturated earth tones with cold blue shadows, shot on ARRI Alexa, 16:9, cinematic color grading, no text, no words, no letters, no logos"

**Conceptual/Metaphor:**
"Single chess king piece on an empty board, dramatic chiaroscuro lighting from above, dust particles visible in the light beam, shallow depth of field, monochromatic with warm amber accent, Kodak Vision3 500T film stock look, 16:9, no text, no words, no letters, no logos"

**Technology/AI:**
"Empty conference room with a single glowing laptop screen illuminating the space, chairs pushed back as if everyone just left, blue screen light reflecting off glass table, atmospheric and slightly eerie, cool desaturated tones, shot on RED Komodo, 16:9, no text, no words, no letters, no logos"
